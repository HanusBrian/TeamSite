Section 1: Demonstration of Knowledge   

 

1.1.1  Manager on duty certified by accredited program

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

The Manager on duty should be certified by an ANSI accredited food safety program or a state-governed program (not SAFE FOOD – for the state of FL).  ServSafe from the National Restaurant Association, National Registry, from Environmental Health Testing,   Prometrics, and 360 Training are the ANSI certified programs.  BBI primarily uses the Skill Soft program (previously known as Mind Leaders) and Prometric.    The certificate must be available and current within the last five years. Cite any local jurisdiction requirement not met such as “a certified manager must be on duty at all times” or “certificate must be posted.” 

Picklists:

·       No manager on duty certified

·       Certification not obtained from accredited program

·       Not on file in the unit

·       Obtained more than five years ago

·       Local jurisdiction requirement not met (add notes)

 

1.1.2  Food Handler Certificates available where required

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

For any restaurants located in a jurisdiction requiring food handler certificates, randomly select three employees and ask to see verification of their food handler cards.  Cite a finding if any one food handler card is missing or expired.  In California only, any employee hired after June 1, 2011 must have a California Food Handler Card 30 days from the date of hire. Riverside, San Bernardino, and San Diego counties have pre-existing food handler card programs and are exempted from the state mandate. Each food facility must maintain records documenting that each employee possesses a valid California Food Handler Card. The cards are valid for three years.  SAFE FOOD certification is acceptable.

Picklists:

·       Facility does not have required documentation

 

1.2.1  Shell stock tags contain proper information and available

Threshold: Cite if any one occurrence is observed.

All BBI brands except Outback, may offer raw, mollusk-type shellfish (clams, oysters, mussels) for sale.   If raw mollusks are sold, ask the manager for the last 90 days of tags. Shellstock Use By Brand:

·       Bonefish: Mussels (regularly), Oysters (regional-NE), Clams (occasional)

·       Fleming’s: Oysters (occasional)

Picklists:

·       No harvester ID

·       No date of harvesting

·       No harvest location

1.2.2  Parasite destruction, feed, and raising information available

Threshold: Cite if any one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

To assure the fish you purchase has been properly handled, a written agreement or statement from your supplier stipulating that the fish supplied are frozen to a temperature and for a time specified or were properly farm-raised and fed must be obtained by the restaurant and retained in their records. All farm raised fish letters are available on the BBI portal, the Manager should show knowledge of where they are located. As long as the supplier and fish remain EXACTLY the same, a letter kept on file (on the BBI portal) is adequate (they do not need to be within 90 days). Sometimes a supplier might use a different processor/supplier, ensure the letter does in fact pertain to the fish in question. If the fish does not pertain to the correct processor/supplier, cite a violation.  Ask to see documentation from on-site freezing or verification of freezing process from suppliers. BBI brands will receive fish that has gone through the freezing process at the supplier or are farm raised.  Raw, raw-marinated, partially cooked, or marinated-partially cooked fish (other than molluscan shellfish) shall be frozen throughout to the following temperatures before service or sale in a ready-to-eat form: 

4°F (-20°C) or below for 7 days (168 hours) in a freezer

OR

-31°F (-35°C) or below for 15 hours in a blast freezer

(Yellowfin also known as Ahi Tuna and other Tuna species and Salmon are excluded. Fish that are farm raised must have documentation stating it was farm raised and what it was fed.)

 All brands carry farm raised fish.  Spot check 1 type of farm raised fish per brand to ensure that they have the correct documentation.  Documentation can be found on the company portal under food safety.

See BBI Photo Guide in LMS Customer Reference Library for list of farm raised fish by brand.

Picklists:

·       Frozen by supplier without documentation

·       Frozen at restaurant without documentation

·       Does not indicate what it was fed

·       Does not indicate that it’s been farm raised

Section 2 – Employee Health

2.1.1  No sick/symptomatic food handler(s) at work-AUTO FAIL

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Employees with symptoms such as vomiting, excessive coughing or sneezing, or diarrhea should not be allowed to work since they may pass pathogens to the customers.  Observe employees for symptoms of illnesses.  While you cannot obviously observe for all symptoms, pay attention to employee conversations that might reference not feeling well, observe for frequent restroom trips, etc.  If you suspect that a sick or symptomatic employee is at work, check with the manager to determine if they have already addressed the concern. Contact EcoSure immediately. 

Picklist:

·       An employee is working while sick or symptomatic and the manager has not properly restricted/excluded them from food service task

 

2.1.2  A documented Health Policy is available, or manager has knowledge of reporting and documentation of exclusion/restriction responsibilities

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

BBI’s health policy is known as “excludable illness policy” is part of a document that the employee fills out upon hire which reviews self-reporting (see below).   They can be found on the BBI portal and do not need to be printed.  All new hires sign this document electronically (since Sept. 2012) during their on-boarding and is part of the employee file.   Ask the manager to see today’s roster of employee’s.  Randomly select 1 associate and ask the manager for the verification form in their file that they have been trained on self-reporting.  If the verification form is unavailable, cite a violation.  As long as they can provide this or the Manager can show knowledge of reporting and documentation of exclusion/restriction responsibilities, mark as acceptable.  The manager should be able to communicate that their health policy is located on the verification form.  If they are unable to do so, ask the following questions:

·       Describe symptoms that would lead you to restrict an employee from food handling and service.

·       Identify the symptoms and diagnosis that require reporting to the health department.

·       If an employee informs you that they were diagnosed with ___, how would you proceed?

 

See BBI Photo Guide in LMS Customer Reference Library for documented Health policy example and employee illness restriction/exclusion guidelines.

Picklists:

·       There is no documented Health policy and the manager cannot demonstrate knowledge of symptoms that would lead to restricting an employee or identify the symptoms and diagnosis that require reporting to the health department

·       There is no documented Health policy and the manager cannot demonstrate knowledge of how to proceed with an employee reporting illness

·       There is no verification in employee files of training on self-reporting

 

2.1.3  Proper procedures for clean-up of vomiting and diarrheal events present and 2 fully stocked bodily fluid clean up kits available  

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Ask the manager to see their 2 fully stocked bodily fluid clean up kits.  The instructions for use should be inside the kit.  These kits should be used for events that involve the discharge of vomitus or fecal matter onto surfaces in the food establishment.  The procedures shall address the specific actions employees must take to minimize the spread of contamination and the exposure of employees, consumers, food, and surfaces to vomitus and/or fecal matter.    If the instructions for use are not present, or there are fewer than 2 kits, cite.

Picklists:

·       No procedures are in place for the clean-up of vomiting and diarrheal events

·       Only 1 bodily fluid clean up kit available

·       No bodily fluid clean up kits available

 

Section 3-Good Hygienic Practices

3.1.1  No smoking, gum or tobacco chewing

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Smoking laws will vary.  Where allowed, it is acceptable in areas designated by the facility; the manager’s office is acceptable as long as the door can be closed to prevent the smoke from drifting into the food service area.  Look for any employees that are smoking or chewing gum/tobacco.  Also look at the floor in the food service and food storage areas for cigarette butts.  Look for cigarette butts, soiled ashtrays, and ashes throughout the kitchen area; including in sinks and floor drains.  Look for ashtrays in the dry storage and cooler areas, as well.

Picklists:

·       Employee observed smoking

·       Employee observed chewing gum

·       Employee observed chewing tobacco

·       Cigarettes/tobacco/butts/ashes in food service area

 

3.1.2  Proper drinking/eating in food service area

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Employees may drink or eat in designated areas, such as a break room or the manager's office.  BBI allows covered beverages with a straw in food service areas away from food. After eating or drinking, even from covered cups, employees must wash their hands before returning to work.  Look for any evidence of eating or uncovered drinks.   Tasting food is important from a quality perspective.  Cooks may taste food during prep by using a single use or clean, sanitized utensil each time.  After tasting, single use utensils should be discarded and reusable utensils must be placed into the ware washing area for proper cleaning and sanitizing and the employee must wash his/her hands before returning to work. 

Exception: Covered drinks with a lid are acceptable anywhere other than in direct contact with food.  Coach to have drinks away from and below food contact surfaces but do not cite.

Picklists:

·       Employees are eating in food prep/service areas

·       There is evidence of eating (partially eaten food or food wrappers) in food service area

·       Employees are drinking from a cup without a lid and straw in food prep/service area

·       Employee drinks are not stored properly

·       Employees are tasting food with soiled utensils or fingers

3.2.2  Proper hair restraints worn in food service area

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Hair restraints must be worn by food handlers at all times in back of house to ensure there is no contamination of food, equipment or utensils by employee hair.  Beard restraints are required for employees with facial hair greater than ¼ of an inch on the line only.  If managers are preparing food, they must also wear a hear restraint.   Proper hair restraints include hats, hair nets, or other hair coverings.  Visors must be worn with a hair net.   

Exceptions: The expo area is exempt from hair restraints.  Servers and managers performing food prep activities in the back of house should have hair restrained such as during pre-opening prep, however food prep activities done in the bar or FOH by servers, bartenders etc. (such as cutting lemons) does not require a hair restraint.

Picklists:

·       An improper hair restraint is being worn

·       A beard guard is not worn when required

Except for a plain ring such as a wedding band, employees may not wear jewelry on their arms and hands when handling or preparing food including watches, medical and religious bracelets.

Section 4-Preventing Contamination by Hands

4.1.1  Proper handwashing procedure

Threshold: Cite for one occurrence observed.

Assessment:

Hand washing must be comprised of specific steps in order to be effective. Improper procedure can lead to soiled hands and the potential for contamination of food, food contact surfaces, and the spreading of communicable diseases.   Dedicate time during the audit to observe employee behaviors, including proper handwashing procedure. 

See BBI Photo Guide in LMS Customer Reference Library for BBI approved handwashing procedure.

Exceptions: Do not time associates handwashing.  If an associate makes a reasonable attempt to wash their hands but it is not the full 20 seconds, use this as a coaching opportunity.  If you observe employees turning off the faucet without a paper towel, coach but do not cite a finding.

Picklists:

·       Improper handwashing technique

·       Only using hand sanitizer or wiping hands on sanitized towel

·       Hands are not properly dried

·       Other

 

4.1.2  Handwashing at appropriate times (other than the bar)

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Neglected hand washing can lead to cross-contamination by unclean hands: If you observe a neglected hand washing situation, it is a critical finding.  Pay attention to the type of situations that are critical for hand washing.  Make sure you observe the whole instance of “neglected hand washing” from the causal behavior to the return to a food handling task.  Be certain that the employee did not wash their hands while out of your range of vision. 

Exception: This question applies to all areas except for the bar.

Picklists:

·       Not washing hands upon entering kitchen

·       Not washing hands when returning to food prep from a break or other tasks

·       Not washing hands after coughing/sneezing

·       Not washing hands after touching face, hair, body or people

·       Hands not washed between glove change

·       Other

4.2.1  When gloves are required, food handlers do not contact ready-to-eat foods with bare hands

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

BBI requires glove use with ready to eat foods at all brands, except for at the bar and servers handling bread (at Carrabba’s only).  If employees are observed handling ready-to-eat foods with bare hands (except for at the bar or servers handling bread – at Carrabba’s), a violation will be cited.  BBI makes no exception to this standard for sushi.

Exceptions: Bar staff garnishing drinks.  Servers handling bread at Carraba’s only.

Picklist:

·       Employee observed having bare-hand contact with ready-to-eat food (other than bar)

 

4.2.2  Single-use gloves used properly

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Disposable gloves should be worn over protective cut-gloves, as well as over any bandage being worn over any unhealed sore, scab, wart, or rash from the wrist down.  If an employee is wearing a non-disposable bandage or apparatus (gauze dressings, elastic bandages, cast or brace) on their hand, that employee should not be allowed to engage in any food prep activity, because hands cannot be properly washed. Food handlers should change their gloves when changing tasks, when they are soiled/torn and at least every four hours with continual use.  If the task is unchanged and the glove change is only to put a new glove, do not cite a violation.  Hands should be washed between glove changes.  Gloves are never to be used in place of hand washing. 

Picklists:

·       Bandaged food handler without glove

·       Gloves removed from hands then reused

·       Hands “washed” with gloves on

·       Gloves not changed between tasks

·       Employee with cast or brace using glove or handling food

 

4.2.3  Fingernails are clean and trimmed on BOH food handlers.  Artificial nails or fingernail polish not allowed.

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Employees responsible for food preparation shall keep their fingernails trimmed, filed, and maintained so the edges and surfaces are cleanable and not rough. BBI does not allow employees handling food to wear fingernail polish or artificial fingernails when working with food.

Picklists:

·       Employee wearing fingernail polish or artificial nails handling food

·       Nails are not trimmed and filed with smooth edges

These sinks are considered “dirty” sinks.  Therefore, they should never be used for washing food/equipment/utensils due to the potential for contamination.  Look for ice, lettuce shreds, straws, lemon wedges, and other debris in the basin.  Identify whether this is a temporary issue or habit in your notes.

Section 6-Protection from Contamination

 

6.1.1  Proper use of food hierarchy in storage

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Raw meat, poultry and seafood items should be stored away from or below fresh produce and ready-to-eat foods when in refrigeration.  Raw food must be stored based upon the minimum internal cooking temperature that is required for each food.  Foods should be stored in the following top-to-bottom order or back-to-front in chef’s drawers and on cold lines:

·       Cooked and ready-to-eat foods, including pasteurized raw shell eggs

·       Whole raw seafood, whole cuts of raw beef and pork (uncooked bacon), raw shell eggs

·       Ground meats or minced fish

·       Raw whole or ground poultry, combination items

 

See BBI Photo Guide in LMS Customer Reference Library for example.

Exceptions: Hierarchy will not be assessed in the freezer at BBI restaurants.  If storage space is limited, side-by-side storage of different items is acceptable provided all precautions are in place to prevent cross-contamination.

Picklists:

·       Improper hierarchy in coolers

·       Other

 

6.1.2  Food stored in packages, covered containers, or wrappings

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

All food should be kept covered, unless it is being actively served, used, or is in the cooling process as long as they are protected from overhead contamination.  BBI considers the line coolers/freezers to be in active use at most times.  If food was left in line coolers/freezers overnight, and not covered, cite. 

Exceptions: If food products in line coolers have recently been stocked, are in the process of being stocked during preparation or are actively in use during meals, do not evaluate for open product. For items being cooled uncovered, open cooling racks with a “roof” as the top shelf or an empty pan in the top slot are acceptable. Exceptions would be whole produce in refrigeration.  Items in chef’s drawers do not need an additional cover if drawer is fully closed after removing item. 

Picklists:

·       Open bags or other packaging are not closed or in a covered container

·       Container holding unwrapped food without a lid

·       Ice bins left open during non-rush hours

·       Cold well/prep line items not covered during off-peak hours

 

6.1.3  Food/supplies stored in appropriate locations

Threshold: Cite for 2 or more instances of food/supplies being stored inappropriately.

Assessment:

All items should be stored properly in designated storage areas.  Food and food supplies should not be in restrooms, garbage areas, mechanical rooms, or other unapproved locations because they are susceptible to contamination in these areas.  Guidelines for proper food storage are:

In a clean, dry location (because moist conditions promote microbial growth).

Away from exposure to splash, dust, or other bacterial contamination.

At least 6” above the floor.  **Use a tape measure if you are unsure of height**

Food contact items should be stored inverted or covered when possible (some equipment and serving ware may not be able to be inverted.  Any containers that store food should be inverted)

Exceptions: Do not cite for straws or stir sticks stored unprotected.  Do not cite for unopened wine/ liquor cases, unopened cases of water bottles, or beer kegs stored on the floor. Also, do not cite for items stored on a dish rack. 

Picklists:

·       Food and food supplies not stored 6” above the floor

·       Food contact items not stored 6” above the floor

·       Food and food supplies stored in a moist/dirty location

·       Food and food supplies stored exposed to splash, dust or other

·       contamination

·       Employee food stored with restaurant food

·       Food and food supplies stored in restrooms/garbage areas

·       Food contact items and single use containers/plastic not inverted or

·       Protected

·       Other

 

6.1.4  Food packages and cans in good condition, damaged products are segregated

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Any cans that are swollen, bulging, sharply dented, have damaged seals, or are rusted should be discarded or separated for return to the supplier.  Observe for any signs of damage or mishandling of food packaging.  Any damaged cans that are received should be labeled for return to the supplier or for destruction/discarding once inventoried (i.e. "Do not use").  If an employee damages a can when opening a case, it should be used first and labeled as such. 

See BBI Photo Guide in LMS Customer Reference Library for example.

Picklists:

·       Packages have holes/tears or evidence of temperature abuse

·       Cans are severely dented

·       Damaged products are not stored separately

·       Other

 

6.1.5  Cross contamination prevented during food preparation

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Cross-contamination of food products must be prevented.  Unpacked foods should be stored away from sources of environmental contamination during food preparation.  Also look for the following:

·       Raw fruits and vegetables must be thoroughly washed and put into clean containers.  BBI brands use water to wash their produce.

·       Raw, whole shell eggs cannot be “pooled” with new eggs added to previous batches.

·       Ice used as a coolant or for food storage should never be used as a food ingredient.

Picklists:

·       Utensils not cleaned/sanitized between preparation of raw and ready-to-eat foods

·       Unpacked foods not protected from contamination during food prep

·       Raw fruits/vegetables not washed

·       Prepped produce placed back in its original container

·       Ice used as coolant is used as food

·       Physical contaminant present such as dust, debris, bottle tops, twist ties, etc.

·       Other

 

6.1.6  Equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces cleaned and sanitized at proper times

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces must be properly cleaned and sanitized at the following times:

·       Before food preparation

·       When going from raw to cooked or RTE products

·       At a minimum of every four hours (or 24 hours if kept ≤ 41°F )

 

If the neglect of cleaning and sanitizing at appropriate times is the direct cause of cross contamination that is observed, give the finding here.

Picklists:

·       Not cleaned and sanitized prior to food preparation

·       Not cleaned and sanitized between raw and cooked or RTE products

·       Not cleaned and sanitized at a minimum of every four hours (or 24 hours if kept ≤ 41°F)

 

6.1.7  Shell eggs not discolored, dirty, have abnormal odor or without small cracks

Threshold: If there are 2 or more cracked eggs, cite a violation.

Assessment:

BBI brands carry shell eggs.  The eggs should be evaluated before being placed in storage for proper condition.  Eggs showing signs of abuse or mishandling should not be accepted.  Check eggs for any discoloration or small cracks in the outer shell (called “checks”).   If an entire sheet within a case or several eggs within a case have small cracks, cite a violation.

Carrabba’s and Outback use shell eggs regularly.  All other brands use shell eggs occasionally.

Picklists:

·       Shell eggs discolored

·       Shell eggs dirty

·       Shell eggs have abnormal odor

·       Small cracks present in shell

·       Damaged products are not stored separately

 

 

6.1.8  Shell stock is reasonably clean, tightly closed and unbroken, no strong fish odor or slimy texture or combined with other lots

7.7.2  Commercially prepared potentially hazardous food in its original container properly dated when opened

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Opened commercially prepared, potentially hazardous food in its original container must be clearly marked to indicate the date that the food was opened.   The product can be used for 7 days from the open date, after which time it is considered expired. 

·        The day the food is opened or prepared counts as DAY 1

·        The date marked as the expiration date may not exceed the manufacturer’s use-by date

Picklist:         

·        Open commercially prepared, potentially hazardous food in its original container not dated when opened

 

7.7.3  No expired potentially hazardous products present

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Look at the expiration dates and holding times marked on a sample of thawing, prepared, and ready-to-eat cold potentially hazardous items to see if they have passed their expiration date.  If they are past their expiration date, but not more than 7 days past their preparation date, coach that this is a quality control concern.  If the product is observed to be greater than 7 days past the preparation date, cite a finding.   Also look at potentially hazardous foods in their original containers (milk, sour cream etc.).  If they are past the manufacturer’s date or greater than 7 days from when they were opened, they are considered expired.   If expiration times are not present and the manager cannot validate expiration date/time, the product will be assumed to be expired.

Picklists:

·        Items are present more than 7 days from the preparation date or open date written on them

·        Items are missing holding times and manager cannot validate that product has not gone past expiration date/time

 

7.8.1  Accurate food thermometer present

Picklists:

·        Food thermometer not present

·        Food thermometer not readily available

·        Food thermometer not calibrated

 

7.9.1  Items properly thawed/slacked

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Frozen items should preferably be moved from the freezer to a refrigerator ahead of time for proper thawing. Unless prohibited by a client, foods may also be thawed using the following methods:

·        Completely submerged, under running water ≤ 70° F

·        In the microwave, but only for small items to be cooked immediately

Picklists:

·        Foods are thawing at room temperature

·        Thawing items are in standing water (at any temperature)

·        Thawing items are in running water > 70° F and no ice is added

·        Thawed in microwave but not cooked immediately

·        Walk-in cooler has an internal thermometer that is not properly calibrated

·        Walk-in cooler has a thermometer that is not properly placed to capture an accurate reading of unit temperatures

·        Reach-in cooler does not have internal thermometer

·        Reach-in cooler has an internal thermometer that is not properly calibrated

·        Dead cockroach(es)

·        3 or more flies not landing in or on food

Assessment:

Storage areas, floor drains, and areas around doors and windows should be inspected for signs of pest activity.  Use your flashlight in these areas.  If you observe any of the below, BBI considers this pest infestation:

·        Cockroaches: 2 or more live cockroaches

·        Flies: 3 or more flies landing in or on food

10.1.3  Insect control devices installed to prevent contamination

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Insect control devices that electrocute or stun flying insects must be properly designed to retain the insect within the device.  Insect lights that attract insects with glue boards inside are acceptable.  Glue strips are also acceptable.  All devices must be installed so that they do not pose a contamination hazard.  They should be placed away from food prep/service areas, utensils, and other food contact surfaces so that insects or insect parts will not fall into food, packaging, utensils or equipment and food contact surfaces and so insects are not attracted toward food-related areas. Dead or trapped insects must be removed from control devices at a frequency that prevents their accumulation, decomposition, or the attraction of additional pests.

Picklists:

·        Insect control devices are not designed to retain the insect within the device

·        Insect control devices are installed over food prep or food storage areas

·        Insect control devices are placed in areas where dead insects may fall from the device onto food, packaging, utensils or equipment and food contact surfaces

·        Insect control devices are not emptied of dead or trapped insects frequently enough to prevent accumulation

 

 

 

10.2.1  Pest activity is prevented through proper sealing of outer openings and the elimination of harborage conditions

Threshold: Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

The facility should be free of pest entryways such as open doors or windows, ill-fitting doors, and holes in the walls/ceiling.  Look around doorways and other openings for gaps that allow in light or are about the size of a pencil.  Any gaps this size or larger can be a potential entryway for pests.

Picklists:

·        Windows are open without screens

·        Windows or door screens are not well sealed or securely in place or are damaged

·        Doors are left propped open when not in use for an active delivery

·        Gaps exist around entryways, pipes, ductwork, etc.

·        Ceiling tiles are missing

·        Cracks or missing tiles in walls are present
