Section 1 – Demonstration of Knowledge

1.1  Allergen sticker present on door and drive-thru, tear pads with allergen and nutrition information available.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Look for allergen stickers at the front door and drive-thru window.  There is not a specific required sign; however one must be present.  One sticker must be present at the main entrance if the restaurant has multiple doors. The allergen sticker may be combined with the location’s hours of operation sticker.

 

Ask the manager to see the allergen/nutrition tear pad, this should be present. The tear pads are color coded and typically change twice per year. The allergen/nutrition tear pad is specific to Arby’s and their products.  Canada: allergen tear pads will not be available in Canadian stores; do not mark this as a violation.

Picklist:

·        Allergen sticker not present on door

·        Allergen sticker not present at drive-thru

·        Tear pads not current

·        Tear pads not present

1.2  At minimum, General Manager ServSafe Certified or local jurisdictional requirement met; food handlers certified when required by local regulation

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

The General Manager is required to be certified by ServSafe or a local equivalent (such as a city food manager card). 

If certification is unavailable, cite a finding and inform the manager if they locate the certificate they have 7 days to send proof to Dr. Papa via fax or email. Where required by local regulation food handlers must have a food handler certificate.  Wallet cards, photo copies of certificates, or any other types of verification paperwork are acceptable.

Only the General Manager is required to be certified.

Electronic copies of certification are acceptable.

Picklist:

·        General Manager not certified

·        Food handler not certified (if required)

·        Certificate not available

·        Certificate Expired

 

 

 

1.3  Beef sheets properly filled out for the previous 7 days. Cleaning and sanitation log maintained. Cold product checks recorded.

Threshold:  Cite if two or more beef sheets are missing. 

Assessment:

Ask the manager for the last 7 days of beef sheets, which include the Cleaning Log section, the Temperature Log section, and the Beef Cooking section.  

All 7 days of beef sheets should be available with all three of these sections completed.

Only cite a finding if 2 days of beef sheets are completely missing.  Or, if 2 or more beef sheets have 2 or more sections completely missing.

For example, if the cold Temperature Log and the Sanitiation Log sections were missing on 2 separate days, you would cite a finding.  However, if only the Temperature Log section was missing on 2 separate days, you would not cite a finding since there was only one section of the log missing on the two days.

If dry lab characteristics are evident on the beef sheets, cite a finding.

Documentation may be recorded electronically.

Picklist:

·        2 or more beef sheets have 2 or more sections missing

·        Two or more beef sheets missing

·        Beef sheets have 'dry lab' characteristics

1.5  Calibrated (ice bath) food thermometers available.

 

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed unless stem thermometers are in use.  More than one occurrence of out of calibration stem thermometers must be observed. 

Assessment:

If a probe system is in place, only verify the calibration of the utilized digital thermometer(s). If a probe system is not in place, verify that all thermometers that are used to check food temperatures are calibrated.

Allow a 2 degree variance. Ice calibration is the preferred method of calibration but any other effective calibration method is acceptable. When stem thermometers are being actively used to take food temperatures, only cite a violation if two or more of the thermometers are out of calibration.

·         A digital thermometer must be on-hand and properly calibrated.

·         If the probe system is being used to cook roasts, then stem thermometers do not need to be calibrated, as if ready for immediate use

·         Arby’s requires at least three stem thermometers, be on-hand and ready for use

Picklist:

·        No thermometers available

·        No digital thermometer available

·        Digital thermometer not calibrated

·        More than one stem thermometer not calibrated

 

 

 

 

 

Section 2 – Employees & Hygienic Practices

2.1 No smoking, gum or tobacco in foodservice area; proper drinking/eating in foodservice area.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Look for any employees that are smoking or chewing gum/tobacco.  Look at the floor in the food-service area and food storage areas for cigarette butts.  Employees may drink or eat in designated areas only, such as a break room, the manager’s office or another set aside area.  They must wash their hands before returning to work because such hand-to-mouth contact is a communicable disease risk. 

 

Look for any evidence of eating or uncovered drinks.  Drinks on a prep shelf or near food or food contact surfaces should be cited as a finding.

Picklist:

·        Smoking observed

·        Chewing gum observed

·        Eating observed in foodservice area

·        Drinking observed in foodservice area

·        Evidence of eating observed in foodservice area

·        Evidence of drinking observed in foodservice area

·        Cigarettes/tobacco in foodservice area

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

2.2   Proper hair restraints are worn in food service areas. No observations of watches or bracelets worn. Rings with stones are covered with a vinyl glove when preparing food. Employees with fake nails and/or nail polish are wearing gloves when preparing food. Nails are of proper length. No earrings with a diameter larger than a quarter.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Hat:

All restaurant employees must wear an Arby’s hat at all times (including visors).  Management personnel must wear an Arby’s hat or hair net when working the backline, preparing food, assembling sandwiches or actively working the fry station. 

Arby’s does not require that a hair net be worn under a hat.

Fingernails:

Maintain clean fingernails at all times.  Fingernails (natural and artificial) should be clean, closely manicured and a reasonable length. Nail polish of any color is acceptable. Employees must wear clean vinyl gloves when preparing food.

Hair/Facial Hair:

Hair must be clean and neatly combed.  Hair, shoulder length or longer, must be restrained back with a rubber band or braided.  Longer hair may need to be double restrained. 

Facial hair can consist of a well-trimmed mustache, beard or goatee.   Long beards or goatees are not permitted.  Beard net is required to be worn over beard when working in prep area or backline. 

Piercings:

Pierced ears are acceptable for male and female employees.  Earrings should not be larger than a quarter in size.

A single facial piercing is allowed.  Facial piercing must be small and studs only, no hoops.

Tongue rings are not acceptable.

Gauges: 

Gauges are prohibited.

Jewelry:

Jewelry is permissible when limited in size and amount, and in accordance with safety and sanitary food-handling standards.   No watches or bracelets may be worn; however, medical bracelets are acceptable as long as proper hand washing occurs.

Rings:

Employees may wear one small ring per hand.  Rings with stones must be covered with a vinyl glove during active food prep or assembly.

Picklist:

·        Employee not wearing proper hair restraint

·        Manager not wearing proper hair restraint during active food prep or assembly

·        Improper hair restraint is being worn

·        A beard guard is not worn when required

·        Employee with multiple facial piercings

·        Gauge observed on employee

·        Dangling earrings larger than a quarter observed on employee

·        Employee wearing multiple rings

·        Ungloved employee wearing a ring with stones during active food prep or assembly

·        Employee wearing watch and/or bracelet during active food prep or assembly

·        Nail polish observed on ungloved employee during active food prep or assembly

·        Fake nails observed on employee-ungloved

·        Nails improper length

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Section 3 – Hand Hygiene Compliance

3.1 Hands washed when required (hourly) and proper handwashing technique observed.

 

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Observe employee behaviors throughout the evaluation to ensure they are washing hands at the appropriate times and using the proper technique. 

You should observe employees wash their hands at least once during the evaluation. 

Coach if an employee doesn’t shut off the water faucet(s) using the paper towel or elbows.

If available, they should apply hand sanitizer at the end of the process.

Picklist:

·        Improper handwashing technique

·        Only using hand sanitizer or wiping hands on sanitized towel

·        Soap not used to wash hands

·        Hands not washed long enough duration

·        Not washing hands when returning to food prep from a break or other tasks

·        Not washing hands after coughing or sneezing, or after touching face, hair, body or people

·        Hands not washed every hour

·        Employee observed washing their hands for less than 15- 20 seconds

 

3.2 Gloves used properly. Food handlers do not contact ready-to-eat foods with bare hands.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

If you observe any employee handling ready-to-eat foods (foods which will not require any further cooking) with their bare hands, cite a finding.

Picklist:

·        Gloves not used when required

·        Bare hand contact observed with ready-to-eat food

·        Hands not washed between glove change

·        Bandaged food handler without glove

·        Gloves removed from hands then reused

·        Hands "washed" with gloves on

·        Skin tone colored band-aids observed worn

Handwashing sinks should be free of excessive dirt, dust and grease buildup. 

Any type of sign, a reminder to wash hands or a handwashing procedure sign is acceptable.

Picklist:

·        Sanitizer dumped in handwashing sink

·        Soap not available in back of house

·        Towels/dryer not available

·        Handwashing reminder signage not present

·        Hot water not available (not 100°)

Section 4 – Food & Packaging Storage

4.1  Proper use of food storage levels in walk-in cooler.

 

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Look for raw roast beef to be properly stored. 

Note: Angus beef, ham, and turkey, corned beef, and smoked brisket are products that arrive pre-cooked.

Cite a violation if ready-to-eat products or produce are beneath raw meat.

Picklist:

·        Improper food storage levels in walk-in cooler

 

 

 

4.2  Food stored in packages, covered containers or wrappings. 

Threshold:  Cite if two or more occurrences are observed. 

Assessment:

All food should be kept covered, unless it is being actively served or used.  This applies to foods in refrigeration units, freezers, hot and cold holding, and dry storage. Open bags should have a closure on them, or should be placed into another container that can be closed. 

For Fry freezer only - lids are needed overnight only.  During business hours, the lids are not required.

 

If only one item is uncovered but the general practice is to cover, coach but do not cite. 

Exception:  Whole produce in refrigeration and items that are in the cooling process as long as they are protected from overhead contamination.

 

Picklist:

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box – walk-in cooler

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box – walk-in freezer

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box overnight – fry freezer

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box – reach-in cooler

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box – hot holding

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box – dry storage

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box – drive-thru area

·        Food product not covered, wrapped, or left unprotected in an open box – fry area

 

 

4.3  Cross-contamination prevented during food preparation.

 

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Equipment, utensils and contact surfaces must be properly cleaned and sanitized at the following times:

·        Before food preparation

·        When going from raw to cooked products

 

Cross contamination of food products must be prevented.  Unpacked foods should be stored away from sources of environmental contamination during food preparation.  Also look for the following:

·        Produce sinks must be cleaned and sanitized prior to use

·        Raw fruits and vegetables must be thoroughly washed (Note: iceberg lettuce, tomatoes, and grapes will need to be rinsed. Leaf lettuce does not need to be washed).

·        Raw, whole shell eggs cannot be “pooled”

·        Ice used as a coolant or for food storage should never be used as a food ingredient

Exception:  Food hierarchy is assessed under question 4.1 Proper use of food storage levels in walk-in cooler.

 

Picklist:

·        Utensils not cleaned/sanitized between preparation of raw and ready-to-eat foods

·        Unpacked foods not protected from contamination during food prep

·        Raw fruits/vegetables not washed

·        Prepped produce placed back in original container

·        Ice used as coolant is used as food

·        Produce sink not cleaned and sanitized prior to use

 

 

4.4  Prepped, ready-to-eat, refrigerated food properly  date labeled.  Commercially prepared food properly dated when opened.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Arby’s requires prepared/thawing/ready-to-eat TCS foods no longer in original packaging to be date-labeled if they are being held overnight. 

 

When assessing labeling practices, please only cite for missing labels on TCS food items that are held overnight in the facility.

 

Spot check 3 items in all storage units; and, if 1 is found unlabeled cite a finding.

Exception:  Sauces and portioned Au Jus powder do not need to be re-labeled with a date.

Fry freezer products don’t need to be labeled.

 

Picklist:

·        Prepared, thawing, or ready-to-eat TCS foods no longer in their original packaging and being held overnight are not dated with preparation or expiration date and/or time

4.6  Food and food packaging stored 6 inches off of floor.

 

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Spot check storage areas for food and food packaging being kept less than 6” off of the floor.

Picklist:

·        Product less than 6” off floor – walk-in cooler

·        Product less than 6” off floor – walk-in freezer

·        Product less than 6” off floor – dry storage

·        Product less than 6” off floor – prep area

·        Product less than 6” off floor – drive-thru area

 

 

4.7  Food, food packaging, and single-use items are stored safely and in good condition. Damaged products and employee food are segregated.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed.

Assessment:

Food packaging must be inverted or covered to protect from contamination. 

 

All items should be stored properly in designated clean and dry storage areas.  Ensure food and food supplies are not exposed to splash from a handsink without a splash guard.  Food and food supplies should not be in restrooms, garbage areas, mechanical rooms, or other unapproved locations because they are susceptible to contamination in these areas. 

 

Storage on milk crates is not permitted. 

 

Employee food should be stored in a segregated area from guest food.

 

Any cans that are swollen, bulging, dented or have damaged seals, or are rusted should be discarded or separated for return to the supplier.  Any damaged cans that are received should be labeled for return to the supplier or for destruction/discarding once inventoried (i.e. "Do not use").  If an employee damages a can when opening a case, it should be used first and labeled as such. 

Picklist:

·        Single use items not stored inverted

·        Food and packaging stored in a moist/dirty location

·        Food and packaging stored exposed to splash, dust or other contamination

·        Employee food not properly segregated from guest food

·        Food and packaging stored in restrooms / garbage areas

·        Packages have holes/tears or evidence of temperature abuse

·        Cheese sauce can severely dented

·        Au jus can severely dented

·        Damaged/recalled products are not segregated

·        Food product less than 12” away from hand sink

They can use the job aid to explain the process – prompt them to do so.

o   Cutting boards in use at prep and sandwich areas

o   Countertops in prep and sandwich assembly areas

o   Food contact utensils in use

Section 12 – Sewage & Plumbing

Section 14 – OSHA

14.1  No ice/moisture buildup around freezers or coolers. No slick floors in other areas.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Check that all floors do not have excess or pooling liquids.  Make sure there is no ice buildup in or near the walk-in freezer or cooler, as well as, ice buildup on the floors.

Picklist:

·        Ice buildup in/near walk-in freezer

·        Moisture buildup in/near walk-in freezer

·        Ice buildup in/near walk-in cooler

·        Moisture buildup in/near walk-in cooler

·        Slick or wet floors observed in BOH

·        Slick or wet floors observed in FOH

 

 

14.2  Safety box cutter is available and used only to open boxes.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

All restaurants should have at least one safety box cutter and it should only be used to open boxes.

Picklist:

·        Safety box cutter not available

·        Safety box cutter not correctly used

·        Something other than a safety box cutter (e.g., knife) was used

 

14.3  "Wet Floor" sign or similar warning devices are readily available and correctly used.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

If mopping is performed during the evaluation, check to see if wet floor signs are being used.  Make sure the signs have been properly placed at the perimeters of the wet floor area if you do not observe mopping while in the restaurant.  If the signs are not used, or if the placement of the signs does not give the customers or employees adequate warning, cite a finding.  Make sure at least one “Wet Floor” sign is present at the restaurant.

Picklist:

·        No warning devices available

·        Warning devices present but not correctly used

 

 

14.4  Employees are wearing slip resistant shoes.

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Check that all employees are wearing the appropriate slip resistant shoes. Shoes must be black and rubber-soled (e.g., Shoes for Crews, Red Wings). 

Picklist:

·        Employee observed not wearing slip resistant shoes

 

 

14.5  Cut resistant gloves are worn when operating slicer or using knife for produce

Threshold:  Cite if one occurrence is observed. 

Assessment:

Check to make sure that when employees are using a slicer to cut produce they are wearing cut-resistant gloves on both hands.  When using a knife to cut produce, only one cut-resistant glove must be worn (on the hand not holding the knife). Vinyl gloves must be worn on top of the cut-resistant glove(s).  When cleaning a slicer both hands must be protected by cut-resistant gloves.

Note: Cut-resistant gloves are necessary when handling produce only.  Do not cite a violation if a protein is being handled without cut-resistant gloves.

Picklist:

·        Cut resistant gloves not worn when slicing produce on the slice slicer

·        Cut resistant gloves not worn when slicing produce using knife

·        Cut resistant gloves not worn when cleaning slicer
